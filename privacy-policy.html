Privacy Policy

Did You Mean? Chrome Extension — Last updated: April 2026

Did You Mean? is a Chrome browser extension that flags personally defined trap words in the Gmail compose window. This policy explains what data the extension does and does not collect.

What data we collect

None. Did You Mean? does not collect, transmit, store externally, or share any personal data, usage data, or any other information about you or your email content.

Local storage

The extension stores your trap word list (up to 8 word pairs) locally in your browser using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage.local API. This data never leaves your device. It is not sent to any server, not shared with any third party, and not accessible to anyone other than you on your own browser.

Gmail access

The extension injects a content script on mail.google.com to monitor the Gmail compose window for words that match your personal trap word list. It does not read, store, or transmit the content of your emails, your inbox, your contacts, or any other Gmail data. The content script operates entirely within your browser.

Permissions used

storage — Used exclusively to save your trap word list locally in the browser between sessions.

activeTab — Used to read the Gmail compose window on the active tab in order to detect trap words. No other tab content is accessed.

host_permissions (mail.google.com) — Required by Chrome's Manifest V3 architecture to run the content script on Gmail. The extension does not access any other domain.

Third parties

Did You Mean? does not use any third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or remote code execution. All extension logic is bundled locally within the extension package.

Children's privacy

This extension is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including minors.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date. Significant changes will be noted in the Chrome Web Store listing.
